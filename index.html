If you're persuing a career in journalism, chances are that you will have to arrange interviews with people of interest in order to get the information that you will need. This webpage will list a step by step guide on how to do this effectivley.

  • Things You'll Need

  • A device for research
  • A device for recording audio
  • Something to write your questions down on
  • something to take notes on during the interview
  • Your undivided attention

  1. Lay the groundwork for the interview.

    To make an interview actually happen, you must set it up obviously. Reaching out through a phone call or via email is the most effective. It is your responsibility to make surre that the subject understands several things: when and where the interview will take place, how long the interview will last, why you are even interviewing them, and what the information will be used for.

  2. Write effective questions.

    Write out a series of questions that are important to the topic you're gathering information on, but also respectful of the person being interviewed. Make sure that your questions are open ended and generate an impactful conversation. Also, the final question should always be "is there anything else that you would like me to know?" or something to that affect.

  3. Begin the interview on a positive note and ask for consent to record.

    Before you ask any questions, attempt to shake their hand and begin a freindly interaction. This is also the time to ask them if they are okay with you recording the audio. If they say no, explain that you're doing so in order to not misquote them. This usually works.

  4. Keep your subject comftorable throughout the interview.

    While you go through your questions, make sure that your subject is at ease and not intimidated. It is vital to establish that you are taking the interview seriously while having an air of professionalism while not making the subject feel uneasy, as this will make them close up and not elaborate as much on their answers.

  5. Conclude the interview correctly

    Once you are done with your final question, make sure that you exit in a proffessional way. Be sure to thank them for their time and express gratitude for their help. I would also recomend sending a followup email afterwords to thank them once again. If you do everything that this webpage says, your research interviews should go very well!
